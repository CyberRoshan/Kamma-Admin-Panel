Low energy lighting

Fitting your home with low energy lighting reduces your energy bills by up to 90% (Kellwood Lighting), with no compromises on quality. Your home’s carbon footprint can also be substantially reduced through this change.

KEY BENEFITS
  • Reduces carbon dioxide emissions
  • Lower energy bills
  • Improve EPC rating
CONSIDERATIONS
  • Possibly rewiring required
ESIMATED COSTS

Switching to low energy lighting can save up to £13 per light bulb per year, reducing the average annual UK electricity bill of £670 (Boiler Guide) significantly. Alongside the direct cut to your energy bills, low energy lighting is cheaper to maintain and run long term.

Description

Low energy lighting was developed as an alternative to the costly and damaging incandescent and halogen lighting options. It comes in two main products: LEDs and CFLs. CFLs use between 70 and 80% (Energy Saving Trust) less electricity than older bulbs, while LEDs are even more efficient. Although they are more expensive to purchase initially, the cost of low energy lighting is far lower in the long term. This is a result of low energy lighting lasting longer and being cheaper to run. These benefits are complemented by the diminished environmental impact. Obviously, the less energy needed to run this lighting immediately reduces the carbon footprint of your home. Low energy lighting also has several other environmental benefits, furthering the advantages of this switch. LEDs in particular reduce light pollution due to their directional light, especially useful in dark sky areas. They also don’t emit UV radiation or contain any mercury, both of which can be damaging.

The relatively easy installation of low energy lighting makes it a viable option for property owners looking to improve their EPC rating at a low cost with no compromises on comfort.
